Inclusive Therapists

By emily
February 3, 2026
Inclusive Therapists is a website to help you find affirming, social justice-oriented mental health providers with certain identities and specialties.

 

 

 

  • "Inclusive Therapists is a social justice and liberation-oriented mental health directory, community, and resource hub. We offer a safer, simpler way to find a therapist, counselor, coach, or mental health provider committed to Racial, 2SLGBTQIA+, Neurodivergence, and Disability Justice.
  • We are a mental health liberation movement creating change through decolonial education, collective care, and activism. Celebrating marginalized identities, abilities, and bodies!
  • Inclusive Therapists does not cooperate with the police or Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). "
  • inspired by the work of Dr. Jennifer Mullan, https://www.decolonizingtherapy.com/
  •  
  • i have used it and found it helpful, esp the identity tags at the bottom that the therapist can choose for themselves
  • VERY thorough & specific searchable identity tags available
  • Service Details tags -- services, specialities, therapeutic approach, advocacy focus
    • has an anti-ABA tag, woohoo! (and Autistic Person Opposing ABA)
    • and Autism-Affirming or Centered Therapy
    • Cultural Resistance Against Systemic Oppression
    • Body Positive
    • Gender-Affirming Care
    • Migrants/Immigrants/Refugees, etc.
    • Disability Justice
    • Black Liberation
    • Land Back, Indigenous Sovereignty
    • Transgender Justice
  • Culture & Identity tags (for the provider themselves) where they can put languages, cultural knowledge, spiritual knowledge, provider's identity
    • helpful for autistic ppl because often in bios, providers say they're "neurodivergent" without being specific (possibly to protect themselves from discrimination sometimes?), but the tags under "Provider's Identity" can sometimes be more specific
  • helps me know who is safe bc they have certain stances/knowledge bases that i also share (e.g. anti-ABA is an extremely good sign)
  • more likely to find decolonized therapy here
  •  
  • has a free matching service form you can fill out? extremely based
  • has mental health resources as well -- anti-carceral care and warmlines that avoid the police: https://www.inclusivetherapists.com/resources
  • they have a podcast
  • and a nonprofit: https://mentalhealthliberation.org/
